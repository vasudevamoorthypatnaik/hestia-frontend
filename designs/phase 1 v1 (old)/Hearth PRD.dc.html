The bet

The unsolved job isn’t displaying a schedule — it’s reducing the mental load of running a household.

Incumbents — Cozi, FamilyWall, Skylight, Maple, Google and Apple Calendar — are sticky but dated. They digitize the calendar; almost none reduce the cognitive labor. This document defines the problems v1 must address, and the requirements to address them. The positioning is an ingestion + delegation engine, not another calendar: AI capture and labor-balancing are the moat, calendar UI is table stakes.

01 The problem

Running a household generates a constant stream of obligations that arrive unstructured — emails, flyers, texts, verbal asks — and must be turned into events, reminders, and tasks, then assigned, remembered, and executed across multiple people, most of whom won’t adopt a new app. Today this labor falls disproportionately on one “household manager,” is invisible, and is poorly supported by existing tools.

02 Pain points, prioritized

What v1 must address, in priority order. Each pain maps to a v1 requirement.

P0 Must address in v1
3.1
The “second account” problem

Families already live in Google/Apple Calendar. A separate app means double-entry or abandonment — “my spouse never opens it” is the top churn driver.

Requirement · Bidirectional, real-time sync with Google & Apple from day one. Additive to existing calendars, never a forced migration.
3.2
Input friction — THE WEDGE

“Early dismissal Friday, pizza day Wednesday, form due Monday” → 3 events + 1 task + 1 reminder. That conversion is the actual work, and it’s manual in every tool.

Requirement · Forward an email, paste text, or photograph a flyer → structured, reviewable events/tasks. The single biggest AI-enabled unlock.
3.3
Invisible & unequal mental load

Apps store events but don’t distribute labor. No incumbent answers “what’s on my plate vs. my partner’s, and is it balanced?”

Requirement · Every item has an owner and an optional responsible adult; a household view surfaces load distribution.
P1 Strongly desired — acceptable in fast-follow
3.4Dumb notifications

Reminders are spam or silence. Need context-aware nudges (“you’re in a meeting until 6 — who’s getting Maya?”) and digest summaries.

3.5Irregular recurrence

Custody (2-2-3, week-on/off) and shift work can’t be expressed by standard RRULE — exactly where coordination matters most. Need a rotation builder.

3.6Kid-activity fragmentation

Activities live in silos — TeamSnap, Remind, Brightwheel, classroom apps — that don’t talk to anything. Email-ingestion is the universal bridge (most send emails); direct integrations later.

P2 Out of scope for v1 — on the roadmap
  • —Meal planning connected to shopping lists and pantry (plan → ingredients → list).
  • —Teen-facing lightweight surface (SMS / shared-link / widget rather than full app adoption).
  • —Smart-display / fridge-screen mode.

03 Evidence from the field

Recurring, documented complaints driving users away from incumbents. These validate the pain points above — treat them as v1 requirements, not nice-to-haves.

Loudest signal
Cozi’s paywall backlash

May 2024: Cozi restricted free users to a rolling 30-day window — impossible to plan summer camp or next season’s sports without paying. Trustpilot fell to ~2.1 stars; long-time users described feeling “scammed” and “held hostage,” locked out of their own data.

Implication · Don’t gate core planning behind a hostile paywall. If freemium, gate on capture volume or premium automation — never on viewing your own family’s schedule.
One-way sync

Cozi only pushes out to Google; changes never flow back, so edits silently desync.

→ Confirms 3.1 — true bidirectional sync is non-negotiable.
No admin roles

No permission levels or locked events — one swipe by a kid wipes a month of entries.

→ Add roles, protected events, and undo to v1.
Data lock-in

No CSV/ICS export — families can’t take years of history with them.

→ Add export & portability; it also lowers adoption anxiety.
Manual entry is hated

Comparison sites headline “stop losing 4+ hours to manual entry.” The feature pulling switchers is AI photo extraction.

→ Validates smart capture (3.2) as the wedge — the market already rewards it.
Ads & clutter

TimeTree users cite intrusive ads on daily opens plus syncing issues.

→ Keep the core clean; don’t monetize via ads in the daily loop.
Feature sprawl

FamilyWall is “overkill”; the least tech-savvy member determines adoption.

→ Argues for ruthless onboarding simplicity over feature breadth.

Sourcing note: patterns corroborated across multiple independent review sources and parenting-community grievances; worth validating with first-party user interviews before locking scope.

04 Goals & non-goals

Goals
  • ✓Become the household’s single source of truth without anyone abandoning their existing calendar.
  • ✓Cut time-to-capture an unstructured obligation into a structured, assigned item by ~80% vs. manual entry.
  • ✓Make the distribution of household labor visible and more equitable.
Non-goals · v1
  • ×Replacing Google/Apple Calendar.
  • ×Being a full project-management or to-do power-tool.
  • ×Forcing every family member onto the app — partial adoption must still deliver value.
  • ×Social/community features.

05 v1 functional requirements

The calendar is the foundation everything attaches to — the bar is “as reliable as Google Calendar, plus the household layer incumbents lack.”

5.1
Calendar sync  P0 FOUNDATION

True two-way sync with Google & Apple/iCloud — edits reflect within ~5 seconds, never silently dropped or duplicated. CalDAV + ICS subscription for read-only external calendars (school, league). Outlook/365 as a fast-follow. Last-write-wins with a visible change log; a clear sync-health surface with one-tap re-auth.

5.2
Smart capture  THE WEDGE

Forward an email, photograph a flyer/permission slip, or paste text/screenshot → AI extracts candidate events/tasks. Extraction captures title, date/time, location, responsible person, prep tasks, and deadlines, with confidence shown. Review-before-commit: nothing auto-commits silently.

5.3
Household model

Members (adults, kids, others) with attributes: has-phone, can-be-assigned, schedule-subject-only. Every event/task carries an owner (whom it concerns — possibly a phone-less child) and a responsible adult (who handles logistics). This split is the hook the mental-load layer reads from.

5.4
Mental-load view

A dashboard of upcoming items grouped by responsible adult, with a simple balance indicator. Not a guilt machine — a visibility tool.

5.5
Shared lists

Basic shared lists (shopping, to-do, packing) with real-time sync — the foundation for later meal-planning connection.

5.6
Smart notifications

A daily/“tomorrow” digest per adult, configurable. Conflict and gap detection — “no one is assigned to pickup Friday.” Reminders route to the responsible adult, not spammed to everyone; respect quiet hours.

5.7
Custody / rotation schedules  P1

A template-driven builder for common patterns (week-on/week-off, 2-2-3, every-other-weekend) that generates the underlying events automatically. The calendar must store and render generated rotation events in P0 even though the builder lands in P1.

P0 acceptance criteria
The calendar must clear this bar before anything ships on top of it.
01A change in Google or Apple appears in-app within ~5s, and vice versa, with no duplication.
02Create a recurring event, edit a single occurrence — both reflect correctly across synced calendars.
03A non-admin cannot delete a protected event; any deletion is recoverable from trash.
04Two conflicting responsibilities for the same adult produce a visible warning.
05An unassigned pickup/event is surfaced as a coverage gap.
06A user can export the full calendar to ICS and re-import without data loss.
07A connected account that loses authorization shows a clear, actionable sync-error state.

06 Key flows

Onboarding

Connect Google/Apple Calendar → confirm imported members → set who can be assigned work.

Capture

Forward school email → review 3 extracted items → confirm → items land on the right calendars with owners and a prep task.

Daily loop

Each adult gets a morning digest of their responsibilities; gaps are flagged for someone to claim.

Rebalance

Household manager opens the load view, sees imbalance, reassigns two pickups to partner in two taps.

07 Success metrics

Activation
% of new households that connect ≥1 external calendar and complete one smart-capture in week 1.
Core value
Smart-captures per household per week — the proxy for offloaded mental load.
Adoption depth
% of households with ≥2 active adult members — beats the “spouse never opens it” failure mode.
Retention
Week-4 and week-12 household retention.
Load equity  ★ NORTH-STAR
Reduction in share of items owned by the single busiest adult over time.

08 Competitive landscape

Positioning: an ingestion + delegation engine, not another calendar. AI capture and labor-balancing are the moat; calendar UI is table stakes.

Product Strength Gap we exploit
Cozi Category default; lists + calendar. Dated; no AI capture; no load balancing; weak sync.
Google / Apple Calendar Already where people live. No household model, no capture, no task/owner layer.
Skylight Hardware display; family-friendly. Display-first; input still manual; hardware lock-in.
FamilyWall / Maple Feature-rich household hubs. Feature sprawl, manual entry, no mental-load framing.

09 Risks & open questions

Risks
  • Sync reliability is existential. A calendar app that loses or duplicates events is dead. Needs heavy investment and testing.
  • Extraction trust. Users must trust AI capture without it silently creating wrong events. Review-before-commit is non-negotiable; measure correction rate.
  • Privacy. Household data (kids, custody, locations) is sensitive. Clear data handling and on-device options should be considered.
Open questions
  • ?iOS-first or cross-platform at launch? (Recommend iOS-first given Apple Calendar & sharing dynamics.)
  • ?Monetization — freemium with capture limits, or flat household subscription?
  • ?How far to push teen engagement in v1 vs. defer to P2?
